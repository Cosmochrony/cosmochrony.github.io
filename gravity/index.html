Newtonian Potentials from Projective Entropy in Three Dimensions

A minimal elliptic-operator mechanism yielding a Newtonian 1/r profile from resolvent-mediated entropy response.

Read the preprint DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18818721

Overview

This article investigates a minimal operator-theoretic route by which a Newtonian long-range potential can arise from the first variation of a projective entropy functional. The setting is purely spatial: a positive elliptic operator in three dimensions.

Defining the projective entropy as $S_\Pi[A]=\tfrac12\log\det' A$, the weak-perturbation response reduces to a resolvent trace, $\delta S_\Pi=\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}(A^{-1}\delta A)$. The spatial structure of the response is therefore governed by the Green kernel of $A$.

Core contributions

  • Projective entropy functional: $S_\Pi[A]=\tfrac12\log\det' A$ defined for positive elliptic $A$.
  • Resolvent-controlled response: $\delta S_\Pi=\tfrac12\mathrm{Tr}(A^{-1}\delta A)$ in the weak regime.
  • Newtonian 1/r profile: in 3D, Laplace-type Green kernels yield a universal $1/r$ decay.
  • Numerical evidence: discrete 3D Laplacian simulations reproduce the predicted long-range response.
  • Coherence threshold: sufficiently extended perturbations generate a macroscopic amplitude shift.

Weak perturbations and resolvent expansion

The analysis is explicitly restricted to the weak-perturbation regime. In this regime, the first-order expansion of the functional determinant provides a controlled approximation of the entropy response in terms of the resolvent of $A$.

This yields a direct link between operator perturbations and spatial response structure, without postulating a fundamental interaction law at the outset.

Numerical verification

The mechanism is demonstrated on a discrete three-dimensional Laplacian with localized symmetric perturbations. Simulations confirm: (i) the validity of the first-order resolvent approximation, (ii) a $1/r$ Green profile, and (iii) a finite spatial coherence threshold required to produce a macroscopic response.

Compact perturbations alter the spectrum locally without generating a measurable long-range effect, whereas sufficiently extended perturbations induce a coherent Newtonian amplitude shift.

Dimensional specificity

The $1/r$ behavior is specific to three spatial dimensions. In other dimensions, Laplace-type Green kernels decay differently, and the entropy variation would not reproduce a Newtonian profile. The result is therefore dimensionally constrained rather than universal across dimension.

Relation to the Cosmochrony program
