Cosmochrony

An exploratory pre-geometric framework in which time ordering, spacetime geometry, and effective quantum phenomena may emerge from the irreversible relaxation of a single substrate χ through non-injective projection.

Read the preprint (Zenodo DOI) Zenodo record DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17957509 ORCID: 0009-0001-7697-7868

Overview

Cosmochrony explores whether several foundational features of physics can be described within a single minimalist picture: a continuous substrate χ whose ordering is intrinsically irreversible. In this view, time is not introduced as a primitive external parameter but is associated with a monotonic relaxation/ordering structure of χ.

A key interpretative distinction is made between the fundamental substrate χ and the effective, spacetime-level scalar descriptor χeff: the latter is a coarse-grained projection used when a stable geometric regime exists. This projection is generally non-injective, meaning distinct underlying structures may correspond to the same (or indistinguishable) effective spacetime description.

The manuscript is intentionally framed as an exploratory program: it aims to provide a coherent conceptual structure and to identify effective limits and possible implications, rather than to present a complete, mathematically exhaustive unification.

Scope statement. This website is a high-level presentation. The authoritative technical reference is the preprint linked above. Claims should be read as hypotheses, effective-limit statements, or research directions unless explicitly proven.

Core hypotheses (high level)

  • Single substrate hypothesis: the framework centers on one continuous, pre-geometric entity χ (not a field propagating on pre-existing spacetime).
  • Projection hierarchy: familiar spacetime quantities are expressed through a projected, effective descriptor χeff that is meaningful only in projectable regimes.
  • Non-injectivity: the mapping χ → χeff is generally non-injective; this underlies the effective nature of observables and constrains what can be reconstructed “under the hood.”
  • Irreversibility: the ordering of χ is intrinsically directed, and this direction is associated with the emergence of time ordering.
  • Localized configurations: stable, localized topological configurations correspond to matter-like excitations (solitonic regimes) with spectral stability signatures.
  • Effective limits: standard equations (e.g., gravitation, electromagnetism, quantum formalism) are approached as regime-dependent effective descriptions under specific assumptions and stability conditions.

The manuscript explicitly distinguishes hypotheses, effective descriptions, and conjectural implications.

What this is / What this is not

  • This is a conceptual and geometric framework intended to be testable in principle.
  • This is a proposal to organize gravity, quantum correlations, and cosmology within a single dynamical picture based on χ.
  • This is not presented as a final “theory of everything” or a complete derivation of all known physics.
  • This is not a claim that all equations are proven from first principles in the current manuscript.
  • This is not a duality claim: spacetime descriptions are effective projections, not an equivalent ontology.

What’s new in Version 1.5 (high level)

  • Sharper ontology of projection: clearer separation between χ and χeff, and explicit emphasis on non-injective projection as a source of effective indeterminacy and observational limitations.
  • Stronger “spectral” unification: increased focus on stability spectra and regime-independent ratios (spectral invariants) as organizing principles across particle and strong-gravity regimes.
  • Particle ↔ black-hole continuity: the preprint explores how the same stability/threshold logic may connect particle-scale bound modes and black-hole-scale relaxation thresholds, with the fine-structure constant α discussed as an emergent spectral signature in the effective description.

These points are presented as conceptual consolidations and research directions; the preprint remains the authoritative technical reference.

Possible implications and avenues for tests

The preprint discusses qualitative implications and points to directions where quantitative development could yield falsifiable bounds. Examples include:

  • Connections between relaxation dynamics and effective cosmological parameters (e.g., Hubble-like behavior).
  • Conditions under which quantum-like correlations arise from shared field configurations (holistic, non-factorizable regimes).
  • Regimes where deviations from standard cosmological scenarios might become observable.
  • Strong-gravity regimes where projection becomes fragile (near deprojection thresholds), potentially affecting wave propagation and effective observables.
  • Spectral programs (numerical/lattice) aiming to extract stability spectra and test whether effective invariants (including α as a candidate signature) can be recovered across regimes.

These items are presented as research directions: turning them into robust predictions requires explicit modelling, parameter estimation, and confrontation with data.
