Cosmochrony

A pre-geometric framework in which time ordering, spacetime geometry, and effective physics emerge from the irreversible relaxation of a single relational substrate χ, through a generally non-injective projection to effective spacetime observables (χeff).

Read the preprint DOI 10.5281/zenodo.17957509 GitHub Repository

Overview

Cosmochrony proposes a minimalist ontological starting point: a single relational substrate χ whose dynamics are intrinsically irreversible. Time is not introduced as an external parameter, but is associated with a directed relaxation (ordering) structure of χ.

A central distinction is drawn between the fundamental substrate χ and effective spacetime-level descriptions, collectively denoted χeff. These effective descriptors are meaningful only in projectable regimes, where stable geometric and causal notions can be consistently defined. The projection from χ to effective observables is generally non-injective: distinct underlying configurations may correspond to operationally indistinguishable spacetime events or measurements, limiting reconstruction “under the hood.”

Dynamical laws are not postulated at the fundamental level but arise from structural constraints on relaxation. In projectable regimes, the effective action admits a Born–Infeld-like form, selected as a canonical representation of bounded relaxation fluxes and causal saturation. This action is not fundamental to the substrate itself, but an auxiliary encoding valid within effective spacetime descriptions. Spacetime geometry is recovered as a continuum representation of microscopic relational connectivity, accessed through spectral reconstruction in appropriate limits.

The manuscript further develops how stable localized configurations of χ give rise to matter-like excitations, and how topological and relaxation constraints underpin effective interactions. In particular, electric charge is interpreted as a chiral–torsional invariant of relaxation fluxes, and several phenomenological consequences are explored, including cosmological and galactic-scale signatures.

Core statements (high level)

  • Single-substrate ontology: one pre-geometric relational entity χ, not a field on spacetime.
  • Projection hierarchy: spacetime quantities arise only in projectable regimes via χeff.
  • Intrinsic irreversibility: time ordering reflects directed relaxation, not an external parameter.
  • Non-injectivity: effective observables generally underdetermine the underlying configuration.
  • Derived dynamics: effective laws emerge from bounded relaxation and saturation constraints.
  • Emergent geometry: spacetime structure encodes relational connectivity in appropriate limits.

Articles and technical notes

The Cosmochrony framework is developed across a small set of focused articles addressing distinct structural aspects. Each article can be read independently while remaining conceptually consistent with the overall framework.

  1. Spectral Reconstruction of Spacetime Geometry
    Relational and spectral derivation of effective metric geometry without postulating a background manifold.
  2. Bell Inequality Violations from Non-Injective Projection
    Structural origin of quantum correlations from non-injective effective descriptions, without invoking dynamical nonlocality or hidden variables.
  3. Born–Infeld Geometry from Bounded Relaxation
    Emergence of non-linear Born–Infeld dynamics and effective spacetime geometry from bounded relational relaxation and flux saturation.

Possible implications and avenues for tests

  • Cosmological behavior emerging from large-scale relaxation regimes, including Hubble-like limits.
  • Galactic-scale signatures (e.g. rotation curves) arising from relaxation constraints without invoking additional matter components.
  • Quantum-like correlations linked to non-factorizable configurations and projection structure.
  • Strong-gravity regimes near deprojection thresholds affecting effective propagation and observables.
  • Numerical and spectral programs aimed at extracting stability spectra and testing effective invariants.
