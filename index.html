Cosmochrony

A pre-geometric framework in which time ordering, spacetime geometry, and effective physics emerge from the irreversible relaxation of a single relational substrate χ, through a generally non-injective projection to χeff.

Read the preprint (Zenodo DOI) Zenodo record GitHub Repository DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17957509 ORCID: 0009-0001-7697-7868

Overview

Cosmochrony proposes a minimalist ontological starting point: a single relational substrate χ whose dynamics are intrinsically irreversible. In this view, time is not a primitive external parameter; it is associated with a directed relaxation (ordering) structure of χ.

A key distinction is made between the fundamental substrate χ and the spacetime-level effective descriptor χeff. The latter is a coarse-grained projection used when a stable geometric regime exists. This projection is generally non-injective, meaning distinct underlying structures can correspond to the same (or operationally indistinguishable) effective spacetime description.

The framework aims to derive dynamical laws from structural constraints on relaxation, and to recover familiar physics as regime-dependent effective descriptions. In particular, the effective action takes a Born–Infeld-like form selected by causal saturation of relaxation fluxes, while spacetime geometry is recovered as a continuum encoding of microscopic connectivity (via spectral reconstruction in appropriate limits).

Core statements (high level)

  • Single-substrate ontology: the framework centers on one pre-geometric relational entity χ, not a field propagating on a pre-existing spacetime manifold.
  • Projection hierarchy: familiar spacetime quantities are expressed through an effective descriptor χeff that is meaningful only in projectable regimes.
  • Non-injectivity and limits of reconstruction: the mapping χ → χeff is generally non-injective; effective observables can underdetermine the underlying configuration, constraining what can be inferred “under the hood.”
  • Intrinsic irreversibility: the relaxation/ordering of χ is directed; time ordering is associated with this direction rather than introduced as an external variable.
  • Derived dynamics: the dynamical law and the effective Born–Infeld-like action are obtained from structural constraints on relaxation and causal saturation of fluxes.
  • Emergent geometry from connectivity: spacetime geometry is recovered as a continuum encoding of microscopic relational connectivity, accessed through spectral methods in appropriate limits.
  • Localized excitations and spectra: stable localized configurations correspond to matter-like excitations characterized by spectral stability signatures, with effective phenomenology depending on regime and projectability.

The manuscript distinguishes proven constructions, effective-limit results, and conjectural implications; this page mirrors that intent at a high level.

Key distinctions (operational)

  • χ vs χeff: χ is fundamental and relational; χeff is an effective scalar descriptor used once a geometric regime exists.
  • cχ vs c: cχ denotes the structural bound associated with pre-temporal relaxation fluxes, while c is the emergent causal constraint within the effective spacetime description.
  • Projection vs deprojection: projectability defines when spacetime observables are well-defined; near deprojection thresholds, effective descriptions can become fragile and must be treated with care.
  • Fundamental vs effective quanta: the manuscript discusses how quantum-like discreteness and constants arise at the effective level, tied to stability spectra and projection structure.

What the framework aims to recover (as effective limits)

Cosmochrony treats familiar physics as regime-dependent effective descriptions of χ dynamics, expected to hold under stability, locality, and projectability conditions. The manuscript develops how gravitational and field-like phenomena can arise as complementary effective manifestations of relaxation constraints and their modulations.

  • General-relativistic behavior as a thermodynamic/continuum limit of relational connectivity, with geometry encoding microscopic structure.
  • Field-like dynamics and wave propagation as effective descriptions of organized relaxation modulations in projectable regimes.
  • Quantum-like phenomena as effective discreteness tied to stability spectra, projection constraints, and non-factorizable regimes.
  • Strong-gravity regimes (including horizon-like thresholds) as domains where projection/deprojection structure becomes central for effective observables.

The detailed statements, assumptions, and derivations are in the preprint; this list summarizes the intended effective-limit recoveries.

Possible implications and avenues for tests

The preprint identifies directions where quantitative development could yield falsifiable constraints. Examples include:

  • Connections between relaxation dynamics and effective cosmological behavior (Hubble-like limits under homogeneous approximations).
  • Conditions under which quantum-like correlations emerge from shared configurations (holistic, non-factorizable regimes) and how non-injectivity bounds reconstruction.
  • Regimes where deviations from standard cosmological scenarios might arise due to projectability structure or relaxation constraints.
  • Strong-gravity regimes near deprojection thresholds, potentially affecting effective wave propagation and observables.
  • Spectral/numerical programs aiming to extract stability spectra and evaluate whether effective invariants can be recovered across regimes.

Turning these directions into robust predictions requires explicit modelling, parameter estimation, and confrontation with data, as emphasized in the manuscript.
