Spectral Admissibility

Modal amplitude bounds in relational Cayley graphs.

Programme overview

Overview

This work introduces spectral admissibility conditions governing relational systems evolving under bounded flux. It shows that the Laplacian spectrum of a relational graph determines the maximal amplitude of each admissible mode.

Core result

The admissible amplitude of a spectral mode with Laplacian eigenvalue λ is

$A^{max}=\frac{c_\chi}{\sqrt{\lambda}}$

This relation establishes a hierarchy of admissible spectral modes determined purely by the relational Laplacian.
