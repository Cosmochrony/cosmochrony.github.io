Critical Coverage and the Saturation Depth Problem

A companion note to O28 that reframes the BFS window-depth asymptotics around a single observable, the critical coverage \(x_1(q)\), with the saturation depth \(n_1(q)\) as a derived quantity.

Read the note Back to the spectral admissibility programme

Overview

The auto-calibrated saturation depth \(n_1(q)\) of the breadth-first exploration of \(\mathrm{Heis}_3(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})\) was the central open asymptotic variable of O25–O28. This note makes two points.

The depth is not a vector-space filling time. At the window edge the cumulative Weil-fingerprint rank is far from full (e.g. \(137/307\) at \(q=307\)), so \(n_1\) is the depth at which the new directions gained per explored vertex drop below a fixed threshold — a stop of marginal novelty, not the completion of a basis.

The asymptotics reduce to one observable. With the ball growth \(|B_n|\sim C_{\mathrm{Heis}}\,n^4\) (\(D=4\)) and the diffusive spectral gap \(\lambda_2\approx 4\pi^2/q^2\), the exact identity \[ n_1(q)=\left(\frac{x_1(q)}{C_{\mathrm{Heis}}}\right)^{1/4}\sqrt{q},\qquad x_1(q)=\frac{|B_{n_1}|}{q^2}, \] collapses the whole asymptotic content of \(n_1\) onto the critical coverage \(x_1(q)\). The question ``what is the law of \(n_1\)?'' becomes ``what is the law of \(x_1(q)\)?''.

Scope statement. This is a framing note: it establishes the reduction and the model-free result \(n_1(q)/q\to 0\), excludes the constant-coverage scenario, and states the open analytical programme for \(x_1(q)\). It does not yet derive that law.

Main points

  • Marginal-novelty reformulation: \(n_1\) is the crossing of the capacity-novelty density \(\sigma(n)=\Delta r(n)/|S_n|\) below a fixed threshold, not a rank count.
  • Exact reduction: \(n_1(q)=(x_1(q)/C_{\mathrm{Heis}})^{1/4}\sqrt{q}\), so the asymptotic law of \(n_1\) is literally the asymptotic law of \(x_1(q)\).
  • Two robust inputs: \(|B_n|\sim C_{\mathrm{Heis}}\,n^4\) (\(D=4\), \(C_{\mathrm{Heis}}\approx 0.427\)) and \(\lambda_2\approx 4\pi^2/q^2\) (diffusive gap of the abelianisation torus).
  • Model-free result: \(n_1(q)/q\to 0\) — the linear law is excluded out of sample (to \(q=601\)).
  • Constant coverage excluded: \(x_1\) falls \(0.61\to 0.15\) over \(q=101\to 601\), ruling out the clean \(\sqrt{q}\) scenario; the surviving readings are sub-\(\sqrt{q}\).

The open problem

Granting the coverage-scaling ansatz \(r_q(n)\approx q\,\Phi(|B_n|/q^2)\), the window criterion \(\sigma(n_1)=\varepsilon_{\mathrm{sat}}\) becomes \[ \Phi'(x_1)=\varepsilon_{\mathrm{sat}}\,q, \] a fixed threshold against an explicit \(1/q\). This single tension is the mathematical front: it forces \(x_1\) to decrease with \(q\) and governs whether that decrease is logarithmic or a residual power.

The open deliverable is a structural law for \(x_1(q)\), derived from a shellwise Weil–BFS novelty estimate controlled by \(\lambda_2\), of which the depth law \(n_1(q)\) is a corollary. The same machinery is the bridge to the per-generation depths \(n_g\).
