Effective Spacetime Geometry from Admissible Non-Injective Projection

Q6b derives the effective Lorentzian geometry from the admissibility filter $\Pi_q$ applied to the relational substrate, inheriting Q5a hypotheses only and providing the geometric framework within which the gauge fields from Q6a propagate.

Overview

Q6b is the geometric companion to Q6a. While Q6a identifies the gauge group $G_\Pi$ from the fibre invariants of the non-injective projection, Q6b analyses the base geometric structure — the effective Lorentzian manifold on which these gauge fields then propagate.

The effective geometry is derived directly from the admissibility filter $\Pi_q$ applied to the relational substrate, without additional hypotheses beyond those of Q5a. The Mosco convergence established in Q5a provides the functional-analytic foundation, and the [H-lift] hypothesis is closed unconditionally by Q9.

Q6b synthesises the geometric output of the BFS stratification programme into a framework suitable for coupling to gauge fields, bridging Q5a/Q5b and the later Q-series papers.

Key role. Q6b provides the Lorentzian geometric background for gauge field propagation, completing the Q5–Q6 pair that jointly underpin Q7 through Q12.

Core contributions

  • Effective geometry from admissibility filter: the admissibility filter $\Pi_q$ selects from the relational substrate the degrees of freedom compatible with spectral admissibility, and Q6b shows that the resulting effective structure carries a Lorentzian metric.
  • Inheritance from Q5a only: unlike Q5b which depends on the O23–O29 BFS stratification analysis, Q6b inherits hypotheses from Q5a only (Mosco convergence of the effective operator). This makes Q6b's conditional status more minimal.
  • [H-lift] closed by Q9: the [H-lift] hypothesis that Q6b carries from Q5a is proved unconditionally in Q9, so Q6b's geometry is unconditional with respect to [H-lift] as well.
  • Gauge field propagation framework: Q6b establishes the geometric framework — the admissible principal bundle $P_{G_\Pi}(M, G_\Pi)$ — in which the gauge fields identified in Q6a propagate. This is the geometric input required by Q12.
  • Bridge to Q5b: while Q5b derives the flat Lorentzian metric from BFS stratification, Q6b provides the broader effective geometry analysis that handles the admissibility filter directly. The two papers are complementary.

Geometry from the admissibility filter

The admissibility filter $\Pi_q$ acts as a projective selection mechanism on the relational substrate, retaining only those configurations compatible with the spectral admissibility constraints. Q6b analyses the geometric structure of the image of this filter.

The key result is that the image carries a Lorentzian metric, inherited from the asymmetry between the central and horizontal directions in the Heisenberg group, as established more concretely in Q5b via BFS stratification. Q6b provides the more abstract formulation that is useful for coupling to gauge fields.

The Mosco convergence of Q5a ensures that the effective operator $L_\mathrm{eff}$ converges in the appropriate functional-analytic sense as $q \to \infty$, giving a well-defined limiting geometry. This is the crucial continuity result that justifies the passage from discrete (finite-$q$) to continuum (limiting) geometry.

Complementarity with Q5b. Q5b derives the Lorentzian metric combinatorially from BFS stratification; Q6b derives it analytically from the admissibility filter. Both give the same Lorentzian signature and are mutually consistent.

Relation to the Cosmochrony programme

Q6b occupies the interface between the geometric programme (Q5a, Q5b) and the dynamical programme (Q7–Q12). Its role is to provide the effective geometric framework that later papers assume:

  • Q7: uses the effective geometry of Q6b to study the spatial bridge problem.
  • Q11: uses Q6b's geometric framework for the co-metric coefficient completion.
  • Q12: the admissible principal bundle from Q6b is the geometric setting for the vertical variation that yields Yang–Mills equations.

Together Q5b and Q6b constitute the complete geometric foundation of the Q-series: Q5b provides the BFS-stratification derivation, Q6b provides the admissibility-filter derivation.

Open directions

  • Curved effective geometry: Q6b establishes the local Lorentzian structure; the global curved effective geometry relevant for the Gravity paper requires further analysis.
  • Coupling to matter: the extension of the effective geometry framework to include projected matter fields is deferred to future work.
  • Gauge–geometry back-reaction: how the gauge fields of Q6a modify the effective geometry of Q6b (the coupled Einstein–Yang–Mills problem) is addressed in Q13.
