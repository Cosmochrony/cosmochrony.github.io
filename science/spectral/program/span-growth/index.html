Capacity Decay Determines Span-Growth Universality on Heisenberg Graphs

A three-branch classification of the cumulative fingerprint span, and the substrate dependence of the cascade-exponent law.

On a single Heisenberg BFS, the exact increment identity and the exact polynomial spheres (homogeneous dimension \(D=4\)) force a complete span-growth classification depending only on \((\delta_c, D)\); the square-root frontier of the cascade-exponent law is identified as expander-specific.

Core result

On a single Heisenberg BFS, the cumulative fingerprint span \(r(n)\) obeys the exact increment identity \(\Delta r(n)=\sigma_c(n)\,|S_n|\), and the exact spheres grow polynomially with homogeneous dimension \(D=4\) (Bass–Guivarc'h). Asymptotic summation forces a three-branch classification depending only on \((\delta_c, D)\):

  • \(\delta_c < D\): polynomial growth \(r(n)\sim n^{D-\delta_c}\) — the Heisenberg capacity-to-growth map is \(\beta_{\mathrm{Heis}} = D-\delta_c\) on this branch;
  • \(\delta_c = D\): logarithmic growth \(r(n)\sim \log n\) (marginal branch);
  • \(\delta_c > D\): saturation \(r_\infty - r(n)\sim n^{-(\delta_c-D)}\), strictly for \(\delta_c > D\).

Two analytic corollaries

  • No valence–exploration proportionality. \(r/|B_n| \to 0\) for every \(\delta_c > 0\): the closure hypothesis of the expander-side growth equation does not transpose to this substrate.
  • No uniform square-root frontier. Since \(r(n)\le|B_n|\), any uniform constant in \(\Delta r \le c\,\sigma_c\sqrt{r}\) must exceed \(|S_n|/\sqrt{|B_n|}\asymp n^{D/2-1}\), which diverges for every polynomial growth dimension \(D > 2\) — the additive \(\tfrac12\) of the cascade-exponent law \(\beta = 1/(\delta+\tfrac12)\) is expander-specific.

Status and open questions

The classification theorem and both corollaries are proved under explicit power-law hypotheses; the numerical exponents are finite-window measurements on the production data (\(q \le 211\)). The window-effective exponents sit near the marginal boundary \(\delta_c \approx D\) — recorded as an observation about window-effective exponents, with the asymptotic branch left open. The polynomial span exponent is distinct from the exponential per-shell rate \(\beta^*\) of the projected-Yukawa mass factorisation; any continued use of the \(1/(\delta+\tfrac12)\) form with Heisenberg-measured exponents rests on a two-substrate identification, to be stated as a hypothesis.
