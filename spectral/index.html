Spectral Reconstruction of Spacetime Geometry

Relational and spectral emergence of effective metric structure without postulating a background spacetime.

Back to Cosmochrony overview

Article overview

This article develops a relational and spectral approach to the emergence of spacetime geometry. Starting from abstract connectivity data encoded in Laplacian operators, it shows how effective notions of distance, dimension, curvature, and metric structure can be reconstructed without assuming a background manifold, coordinates, or fundamental geometric degrees of freedom.

The construction is intentionally kinematical. No gravitational dynamics or field equations are postulated. Geometric quantities arise only as effective descriptors in regimes where the relational substrate admits a stable and spectrally admissible continuum approximation.

Key results

  • Definition of operational spectral distances derived from Laplacian eigenmodes.
  • Criteria for spectral admissibility and the emergence of continuum geometry.
  • Reconstruction of effective metric structure via local quadratic approximations.
  • Recovery of Schwarzschild-type geometry as a kinematical consistency limit.
  • Identification of robust spectral invariants constraining admissible geometries.
